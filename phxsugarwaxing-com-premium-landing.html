ANCIENT METHOD • MODERN BENEFITS

The natural, gentle hair removal clients prefer

Sugar waxing (also known as sugaring or Persian waxing) is an ancient, natural hair removal method that uses a paste made primarily of sugar, lemon juice, and water. Used since 1900 BC in regions surrounding Persia and Egypt, it is applied at room or body temperature — eliminating the risk of burns associated with hot wax.

Gentle on Sensitive Skin

The sugar paste adheres only to dead skin cells and hair rather than live skin. This makes it generally less painful and safer for sensitive skin while also acting as a gentle exfoliant.

Reduces Ingrown Hairs

Applied against the direction of hair growth and flicked off in the direction of growth. This technique reduces hair breakage, minimizes ingrown hairs, and causes less trauma to the skin than traditional waxing.

Long-Lasting Results

Provides smooth, hairless skin for 4 to 6 weeks. With consistent treatment, many clients experience finer, sparser hair growth over time. Ideal hair length is about 1/4 inch.

Natural Ingredients & Easy Cleanup

The water-soluble paste is easily cleaned with warm water and leaves no sticky residue. It contains no harsh chemicals, resins, or parabens found in many conventional waxes.
